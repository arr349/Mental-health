Anxiety Coping Methods

  • Question your thoughts - Negative thoughts or thought distortions are common in people suffering from anxiety.
  • Deep Breating - Try box breathing! Take one breath for 4 counts. Hold your breath. Then breathe out for 4 counts.
  • Write down your thoughts - Writing down how you feel in a journal is a good way to let loose and get out of your head.
  • Focus on what you can control - There are somethings in life that you simply can't control. Whether it be an unexpected circumstance or something that someone said. Accept that you can ultimately only control yourself.
  • Grounding - Focus on one inanimate immovable object, such as a wall, clock, or photo. Concentrate only on that object for 10 seconds. Then do it again but for 9 seconds. Then again for 8 seconds, then 7 seconds, until you reach 1 second. There are a lot more grounding techniques you can try.
  • Think positive - In our world of constant stress and external pressures, it can be easy to dwell on past failures or regret on what could have happened. Instead of having a pessimistic view on life, try to think about the bright side! Instead of a challenge, think of an oppurtunity.
