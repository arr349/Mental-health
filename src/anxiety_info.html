Anxiety Information

Overview

The American Psychiatric Association (APA) defines anxiety as "an emotion characterized by feelings of tension, worried thoughts, and physical changes."

It's completely natural to feel anxiety from time to time, we all get anxious at some point! However, when anxiety starts interfering with other parts of your life, such as work, school, or relationships, that's when it becomes a problem.

For someone with an anxiety disorder, anxiety is a debilitating part of everyday life. As the National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) puts it: "the anxiety does not go away and can get worse over time".

People with anxiety disorders may have thought distortions or intrusive thoughts of constant worry. The APA says: "They may also avoid certain situations out of worry." Physical symptoms such as a rapid heartbeat, sweating, shaking, or dizziness are common.

List of Anxiety Disorders

  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder
  • Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
  • Panic Disorder
  • Post Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  • Social Anxiety Disorder
